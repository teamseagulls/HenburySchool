Outreach Classrooms

Brief background

The first Outreach classroom was opened at CSC in the early 1990’s. Programs offered to students are determined by student’s individual Education Adjusted Plans. Students attending the Outreach classrooms often are more independent, able to be independent in self-care and display safe and appropriate behaviours. All of these classes have a major focus on Literacy and Numeracy. When students reach senior school at Darwin or Casuarina Outreach they have the opportunity to access Vocational Education and Training (VET) programs with a major push towards gaining employment at the completion of their final year of schooling.

Objectives

Outreach Programs accommodate the specific learning needs of learners with intellectual disabilities at Casuarina Senior College (CSC), Darwin High School, Sanderson Middle School, Nightcliff Middle School and Dripstone Middle School. This occurs with the collaboration of the 4 mainstream schools. Henbury has a physical space within these schools, where Henbury programs are taught but where there is also inclusion with the mainstream school. Outreach classes operate in conjunction with the mainstream school allowing Henbury students the possibility of accessing the host school’s facilities for suitable lessons and activities, and for Henbury students to socialise with mainstream students and staff.
