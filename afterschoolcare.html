Outside School Hours Care (OSHC)

Henbury OSHC was established in 2008 by members of Henbury School Council in response to parent need for care for children after school and during the school holidays. Start-up numbers for OSHC was 6 children, with the Holiday Program starting 5 months later and caring for 10 children. Approved numbers as of 2010 are 15 for OSHC, and 20 for Holiday Program.

Philosophy

Henbury Outside School Hours Care is a child-focused place where:
children and families and staff are treated as valued individuals the value of play is important and children have opportunities for challenge children are encouraged to develop to their full potential within a safe, caring and supportive environment.

Henbury Outside School Hours Care operates according to a philosophy of open management and aims to reflect the local community by encouraging participation and discussion about all issues relevant to the running of the service.
